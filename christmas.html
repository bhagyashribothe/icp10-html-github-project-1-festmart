CHRISTMAS

Christmas is an annual festival commemorating the birth of Jesus Christ, observed primarily on December 25 as a religious and cultural celebration among billions of people around the world. Christmas Day is a public holiday in many countries, is celebrated religiously by a majority of Christians, as well as culturally by many non-Christians, and forms an integral part of the holiday season surrounding it.

JESUS :-

Jesus Christ is a religious leader and central figure in Christianity, one of the world's major religions: Birth: Jesus was born in Bethlehem.
Teachings: Jesus taught people to live a good life, forgive others, and honor God. He often used parables, or short stories, to illustrate his message.
Miracles: The Gospels describe many miracles Jesus performed, including raising the dead, turning water into wine, and healing the sick.
Crucifixion: Jesus was crucified in Jerusalem around 30 CE. According to the Bible, he was hung between two thieves and a sign was affixed to the cross that read "Jesus of Nazareth, King of the Jews".
Resurrection: According to Christian belief, Jesus rose from the dead on the third day after his crucifixion.
Ascension: Jesus ascended back to God the Father 40 days after his resurrection.

CELEBRATION :-

Decorations: Christmas trees are decorated with lights, tinsel, and other ornaments. A wreath of leaves or pine is placed on the front door. Holly is used for decoration, and mistletoe is hung in the center of a room.
Gift-giving: Gifts are exchanged under the Christmas tree.
Food: Traditional Christmas foods include turkey, ham, meatballs, herring, goose, pork, and beef. Eggnog is a sweetened dairy-based beverage that is often garnished with cinnamon or nutmeg.
Music: Carols are played.
